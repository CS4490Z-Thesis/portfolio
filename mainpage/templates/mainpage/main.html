ABOUT ME

I am aware I have quite a long name, but it is built from the Arabic words Abd, ul- and Rahman. The name means "servant of the most gracious", ar-Rahman being one of the names of God in the Qur'an. Feel free to call me ‘Abdul’ in short form if you’d like.

My name is linked to a big part of my character. I would characterize myself as religious and spiritual, and I aim to serve God in all matters of life. Even my producing of quality code is a way in which I hope to serve God, as to serve God is to serve His creation in my view.

Other subjects I am interested in are: philosophy, literature and depth psychology.

Of course, I am also passionate about software development which itself is a big parent topic to many other subjects. I am particularly interested in web development (although I am open to operating in different developmental domains).

All throughout my childhood I have been consistently glued to a computer monitor. I was always amazed on how exactly all of these images and sounds emanated from a screen, as I appreciated it so very much. Most of my fondest childhood memories are connected to some sort of product developed via code.

And so I consider it not only my pleasure, but my honour to implement and produce code onto the collective world wide web. It is my payment for all of the amusements, joys, and ease I've received from the hard work of other developers.

I enjoy the collaborative nature of working on a team building a product. The feedback, discussion, and review in this environment brings out the best in each individual member, and is a crucial component in my own personal development. And so I am currently looking for employment opportunities in an established company.

MY EXPERIENCE

I’ve worked a variety of jobs over the past couple of years. My first job was working at the Schulich School of Medicine & Dentistry as a computer technician. I diagnosed, troubleshooted and repaired a variety of computer systems; working on both hardware and network issues. I also performed hardware and software installations, imaging, deployments, and malware/virus cleanup of computers. Although this job did not require programming, I still learnt a lot regarding computer organization/architecture, which I found to be a great boon to my computer software skills.

I then worked at the Brain and Mind Institute as a computer programmer for the summer. I developed an application using MATLAB to program experiments to measure the human inclination to impose a rhythmic and temporal structure onto a variety of different contextual intervals (silence, beats, noisy stimuli)

My most comprehensive job experience would be my 16 month internship at Autodata Solutions. I had the opportunity to experience what it was truly like to work in the thick of the software development industry, as I served a company housing big clients such as Ford, GM, Nissan, etc. I had the privilege of being able to contribute to all phases of the software development lifecycle: analysis, design, implementation, verification, and maintenance of software solutions.

My last and biggest software development experience within university would be from my year long thesis project I completed for my Honors Specialization in Computer Science at Western. The project was to build a web-based platform that was to be accessed simultaneously by multiple computers to be used to present video media to research participants in different “free-choice” research paradigms. Although I was in the service of my thesis supervisor and coordinator, I was given a great deal of autonomy in regards to how to develop the research project, and I was tasked with implementing the full stack of the application. After a long year, I finally completed the full-fledged asynchronous web application from the ground up to my supervisors’ praise.

I am currently doing freelance software development. I enjoy building anything from small business sites and landing pages to portfolio themes and rich interactive web applications. The idea that I could make a client's vision for their product come true through my programming expertise brings me lots of joy and makes me enjoy 'working'. So far I have worked with one client by the name of 'Drive Buy Auto Sales' and have successfully completed their product. In my past job experiences I worked with proprietary code and so I cannot display my past works to you; but the application I built for this dealership is public - so you can check it out here!
